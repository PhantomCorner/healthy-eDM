Integrating VAZKEPA® Into CV Risk
Reduction Strategies: Practical
Experience and Guidance
Tuesday 20 May 2025 | 7.00pm – 9.00pm
Adelaide Marriott Hotel (Archive Room)
Level 1, 141 King William Street, Adelaide SA 5000
VAZKEPA® is an ethyl ester of eicosapentaenoic acid (EPA), indicated to reduce the risk of cardiovascular (CV) events in high CV risk, statin-treated patients with elevated triglycerides (≥1.7 mmol/L), and is now PBS listed.1,2 Join us for an insightful discussion on triglyceride-associated residual risk and the current landscape of triglyceride management in Australia.
We will explore the science behind VAZKEPA® and key findings from the REDUCE-IT study,3 followed by practical guidance on integrating VAZKEPA® into CV risk reduction strategies. The session will also cover PBS criteria and an interactive real-world case study, providing actionable insights for your clinical practice.

speakers

Dr Subodh Verma

MD, PhD, FRCSC, FAHA, FCCS, FCAHS

Dr Subodh Verma is a cardiac surgeon-scientist, Full Professor, and the Canada Research Chair in Cardiovascular Surgery. He is a Fellow of the Canadian Academy of Health Sciences, a past member of the Royal Society of Canada College of New Scholars, Artists and Scientists, and a past recipient of the Royal College of Physicians and Surgeons of Canada Gold Medal in Surgery. Dr Verma has actively contributed to Canadian and American clinical practice guidelines, and he had/has leadership roles in numerous contemporary global heart failure, cardiometabolic disease and atherosclerosis trials.

Prof Ian Hamilton-Craig

MB, BS (Adel), PhD (McMaster), FRACP, FCSANZ, FLS (Lond)

Professor of Preventive Cardiology, Flinders University School of Medicine and Emeritus Professor of Medicine, Griffith University School of Medicine. Prof Hamilton-Craig s clinical interest concerns genetic lipid disorders, particularly familial hypercholesterolaemia (FH) and statin intolerance. He is director of Lipid Clinics SA, chair of the SA Lipid Group, and a member of the CSANZ Cardiovascular Genetics Council, FH-Australasia Network of the Australian Atherosclerosis Society, the US National Lipid Association and the Preventive Cardiology Council of the European Society of Cardiology
